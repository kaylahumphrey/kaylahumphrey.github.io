Intro

Welcome!

My name is Kayla Humphrey, and I recently graduated from the Professional Writing Certificate program at Cal State Long Beach. I am an aspiring technical writer with a strong foundation in writing user manuals, proposals, and memos and editing technical content.

While I’m still early in my career, I’m excited to keep learning and growing as a writer. I enjoy organizing information in a clear and helpful manner. I’m always up for a challenge. I aim to become a reliable and creative asset to any team that values thoughtful, user-focused documentation.

Work

User Manual: Adobe Photoshop

View Sample

The sample is a manual I created for Adobe Photoshop. It was my final project for the Professional Writing Program at California State University Long Beach. The primary audience would be beginners who want to begin using Photoshop but need help knowing where to start. The guide is split into two parts. The first part details Photoshop's user interface before explaining core beginner concepts of masks and layers. The second part of the guide covers different editing techniques beginners can use for their Photoshop projects.


User Manual: Canon

View Sample

The sample is a manual I wrote for my manual writing class at California State University Long Beach. The manual is written for the Canon TS6420 printer. The manual aims to provide an easy and understandable setup while showing off the printer's different features. The manual is divided into four sections: Overview, Setup, Features, and Maintenance. The typography style was made so that the sections stood out from each other and to prevent a cluttered appearance in the overall manual.


Technical Editing: Comprehensive Edit

View Sample

The sample is a comprehensive editing assignment I made for my technical editing class at California State University Long Beach. The assignment involved a memo, a style guide, and editing at least 25 pages.I performed a medium edit of the first two chapters of Shelby Mahurin's "Serpent & Dove" for my assignment. I did the work using Track Change in Microsoft Word. The memo was designed to tell the author my evaluation of her work's content, style, and mechanics before detailing my editing plan. The style guide was created to keep track of mechanics, visuals, and specialized terms consistent throughout the work. I listed the author's queries separately from the work so they did not crowd the side of the corresponding page and could be repeated if necessary.


User Manual: Playstation Network

View Sample

The sample is a short manual I wrote for my manual writing class at California State University Long Beach. The guide covers setting up the PlayStation network on your console. The assignment consisted of two parts which involved writing the guide before coding it in XML. Two copies of the guide will be part of the sample. The first copy will show the code used while the second will show the final result. The professor of the class provided the Document Type Definition (DTD), which defines the XML elements, that is located at the top of the page in the first copy. However, I was responsible for adding the proper elements to the rest of the guide and ensuring that the code worked correctly.


Proposal Writing: Internal Memo

View Sample

The following sample is an internal memo I wrote for my proposal writing class at California State University Long Beach. It covers a hypothetical situation where I work for a security company and propose hiring a technical writer for their engineering team to the higher-ups. I named the company Aegis Security Systems, which is mentioned by name a few times throughout the proposal. I addressed it to the CEO of the company and the engineering manager. The names used for both are authors from some of my favorite books. The angle is that the company's growing reputation in its field generates more job requests. The current situation section further explains the company's recent struggles with these changes.


Proposal Writing: External Memo

View Sample

The sample is an external memo I wrote for my proposal writing class at California State University Long Beach. The memo covers a hypothetical situation that is based on work experience. I propose that a smaller business upgrade its point-of-sale systems at its registers.A point-of-sale device is a machine customers use when they want to pay with a card. I use the shortened term POS for point-of-sale throughout the proposal. As a hypothetical situation based on work experience, I took an approach as the software company representative. From this approach, I framed the current situation around problems brought up in incident reports concerning the older models, focusing on its system crashes and issues with card scammers. From there, I have a project plan involving a two-day installation plan of the newer models to reduce the interruption it may cause to the business.


Proposal Writing: RFP Analysis

View Sample

The following sample is an analysis I created of an RFP (Request for Proposal) for my proposal writing class at California State University Long Beach. The RFP was for the National Digital Newspaper Program, a collaborative program between the Library of Congress and the National Endowment for the Humanities. funding opportunity number is 20230112-PJ, posted on September 14th, 2022. I begin my analysis by summarizing the funding opportunity before providing background information on both institutions. After my summaries, I recommend potential steps to be taken if we decide to accept the funding opportunity.

About Me

Originally from Riverside, California, I am now based in Orange County. I earned my Bachelor of Arts in English from Concordia University Irvine before completing a post-graduate certificate in Professional Writing at California State University Long Beach. I pursued this path with the goal of becoming a technical writer because I genuinely enjoy transforming complex ideas into simple, accessible documentation.

In addition to the various writing styles I studied in the writing program which ranges from manual and proposal writing to technical editing. I have also gained hands-on experience with tools such as Visual Studio Code, Microsoft 365, and Adobe Photoshop. I am also well-versed in MLA and Chicago-style formatting, and I bring a detail-oriented, adaptable mindset to every project I take on.

I am always eager to learn and grow. Whether it is mastering a new platform, improving my editing techniques, or exploring better ways to communicate information, I am constantly looking for ways to sharpen my skills and deliver value through my work.
